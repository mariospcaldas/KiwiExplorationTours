Cultural Values

Value Description
Manaakitanga Hospitality and kindness shown to others. Central to Māori society.
Whanaungatanga Building relationships through shared experiences.
Kaitiakitanga Guardianship of the environment and natural resources.
Wairuatanga Spirituality and connection to the natural world and ancestors.

Whānau

In the Māori language, "whānau" refers to extended family, family group, or a familiar term of address for a group of people. It encompasses a wider concept of family than just the immediate nuclear family, often including close friends and community members.

Marai

A marae is a fenced-in complex of carved buildings and grounds that belongs to a particular iwi (tribe), hapū (sub-tribe) or whānau (family). Māori see their marae as their tūrangawaewae - their place to stand and belong.

Pounamu

Pounamu (also known as greenstone or jade) is a valuable and culturally significant material in Māori traditions, revered for its beauty, strength, and spiritual connection. Pounamu is considered a sacred stone with its own spirit, linked to mountains and the land itself.

Haka

Haka is a traditional Māori dance form, often performed in a group and using loud chanting, facial expressions, and body movements like foot stamping and hand slapping. It is a way to express pride, strength, and unity.
